About Me

My name is Matthew (Matt) Richards and I am a data science fellow at Insight Data Science. This prediction model and web application are part of my project at Insight for the Winter 2018 session.

Prior to joining Insight, I worked as a postdoctoral fellow at the Institute for Systems Biology. As a member of the Hood-Price Lab, my work was focused on building tools and resources to better understand how genes interact with one another. One of those tools was TReNA, a Bioconductor package that provides an interface for filtering genes and selecting features with different regression methods. To support this package, I built 108 PostgreSQL databases containing DNA footprints for 27 different human tissues. These databases were also the basis for performing machine learning using logistic regression and gradient boosted classification to differentiate DNA binding sites. I also contributed to the MotifDb package on Bioconductor, which compiles information on DNA binding motifs from a plethora of sources.

When I'm not working with data, I like to keep active by playing all sorts of sports and hiking various trails near Seattle, WA. I thoroughly enjoy making and eating tasty food, and I have made it my mission to explore and rate the best bakeries in Seattle.
